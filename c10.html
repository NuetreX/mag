UDAAN (RC Drone competition)

UDAAN

Join us for an exhilarating showcase of drone technology and innovation at Udaan an RC Drone Competition. This thrilling event brings together drone enthusiasts, students, and professionals from all walks of life to demonstrate their skills, creativity, and ingenuity in a series of captivating challenges.

Eligibility Criteria:

● Participants should be from any UG course.
Level 1: Basic Presentation & Flying
● Participants kick off the competition with informative presentations covering essential drone components, terminology, and safety measures.
● Participants demonstrate their takeoff, landing, and basic flight maneuvers with precision and control.

Judging Criteria:

1. Presentation (20 points):
- Clarity: How well the participant explains key drone components, terminology, and safety measures.
- Engagement: Ability to communicate effectively and engage the audience.
2. Pre-flight Checks and Safety (10 points):
- Knowledge: Understanding of pre-flight checks, safety guidelines, and regulations.
- Demonstration: Properly inspecting the drone, checking battery levels, and assessing flight conditions.
3. Flight Skills (20 points):
- Takeoff and Landing: Smooth takeoff and controlled landing.
- Basic Maneuvers: Execution of fundamental flight movements (hovering, forward/backward motion, turns).
- Control: Demonstrating control over the drone's position and orientation.
- This is an elimination round to see if the user has moderate control over the drone.

Level 2: Range of Drone and Motor Control through path

● Participants navigate their drones through a series of intricate flight paths, showcasing translation, pitch, and yaw motions. Judging Criteria:
1. Motor Control (15 points):
- Translation Motion: Executing controlled lateral movements (left/right, forward/backward).
- Pitch Motion: Skillful tilting of the drone's nose up or down.
- Yaw Motion: Controlled rotation of the drone around its vertical axis.
2. Range of Flight (20 points):
- Altitude Control: Maintaining consistent flight altitude during various movements.
- Distance Control: Demonstrating controlled flight over a designated range.
- Smooth Transitions: Achieving seamless transitions between different flight directions.
3. Stability and Precision (10 points):
- Consistency: Maintaining stable flight during different maneuvers.
- Accuracy: Precise execution of motor commands and achieving intended flight paths.
4. Time (5 points):
- The team is judged on the time taken to complete the track.

Level 3: Weight lifting or air show

● Participants fly the drones with attached weight which would be provided.
● Drones perform graceful aerial ballets, displaying a remarkable range of flight patterns and showcasing the pilots' finesse and control.
Judging Criteria:
Remember, these judging criteria are adaptable based on the specific goals and audience of your competition. Ensure that participants are well-informed about the criteria beforehand to promote transparency and fairness in the evaluation process.
1. Weight lifting (20 points):
- The team is judged on the range of motion and time on air while carrying 200g of weight.
2. Air show (20 points):
- The team is given 3 minutes of time to show anything of their choice with the drone on air.

REGISTRATION FEE: 350/-

Team Size: 4-5 members

Rules

Rules and Regulations: 1. The drone is required to have a minimum of 3 rotors and a maximum of 6 rotors.
2. The diagonal length of the drone must not exceed 40 cm.
3. Drone flights should not disrupt or interfere with other participants or activities within the competition area.
4. Participants must maintain safe distances from people, animals, and property throughout the entire duration of the competition.
5. Participants bear the responsibility for the safe operation of their drones and are obligated to promptly report any technical issues.
6. Flying outside of the designated areas is strictly prohibited and may result in penalties or disqualification.
7. Participants must adhere to the specified time limits for both their presentations and flight performances.
8. Negative Points: A deduction of 5 points will be incurred for each instance of a crash.
9. The decision of the judges is deemed final.
