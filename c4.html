Spark-A-Thon

Ignite Innovation, Ignite Success

Get ready for a dynamic and innovative competition that transcends traditional boundaries — the Spark-A-Thon! Brought to you by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, this event is designed to ignite creativity, problem-solving, and collaboration.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:

Participants from any UG course.

EVENT LEVELS:

ROUND 1
Each team is required to present an abstract of their idea based on any one of the given themes. In the abstract, only the crucial points should be highlighted. The word limit for the submission is 150 words. Top teams meeting the criteria will qualify for the next round.
ROUND 2
Teams selected for the second round are mandated to submit a comprehensive proposal. The proposal should encompass an executive summary, a needs analysis or secondary survey, the scope of the idea, a stepwise implementation plan, impact analysis of the idea, a timeline for execution, and references. The word limit for the submission is set at 750 words. Ensure that your proposal provides a detailed and well-articulated overview of your idea, addressing each component thoroughly.
· Participants are required to present their idea using a PPT.
· Plagiarism in any form is strictly prohibited.
· Entries will be judged on the basis of logical flow, analytical reasoning, innovative solutions, and presentation.
· Only submissions made by the deadline will be considered for the competition.
Abstract (Round 1):
● Relevance to the chosen theme
● Clarity and conciseness
● Highlighting crucial points within the 150-word limit
Proposal (Round 2):
● Executive Summary: Brief overview capturing the essence of the idea
● Needs Analysis or Secondary Survey: Demonstration of understanding the problem context
● Scope of the Idea: Clear definition of the idea's boundaries and objectives
● Stepwise Implementation Plan: Well-structured plan outlining how the idea will be executed
● Impact Analysis: Evaluation of potential positive outcomes and effects
● Timeline for Execution: Realistic and well-defined schedule for implementing the idea
Presentation (Round 2):
● Quality of the PPT: Clarity, organization, and visual appeal
● Oral Presentation: Communication skills, articulation, and ability to answer questions

EVENT DURATION:

Round 1: Abstract : 45 minutes - 1 hour Teams must submit a 150-word abstract highlighting crucial points of their idea, based on one of the provided themes, as part of the Spark-A-Thon competition's first round. Round 2: Comprehensive Proposal Guidelines: 1 hour - 1.5 hour The proposal for the Spark-A-Thon's second round must be a comprehensive document within a 750-word limit, covering an executive summary, needs analysis, scope, stepwise implementation plan, impact analysis, execution timeline, and references. Overall Event Duration: Approximately 2.5 - 3.5 hours

REGISTRATION FEE: 300/-

TEAM SIZE: 1-4 members

SUGGESTED VENUE: Block 2, Ground Floor.

Rules

TEAM SPECIFIC RULES:
· All participants are required to carry their college ID cards.
· Participants can register either individually or in teams of 2 to 4 members.
· The competition will be conducted in two rounds.
· Participants will not be able to switch teams once the tournament has begun.
