ProSolve Show

ProSolve Show: Innovate, Create, Dominate!

It is time to unleash the entrepreneur in you. Design and create a solution to day-to-day problems using modern technology and creative thinking. Teams are not only tasked with a prototype/concept but also have to craft a compelling advertising strategy followed by an ultimate presentation wherein the team will be posed with questions and must defend their creations. Welcome to the ultimate showdown of creative minds at ProSolve Show!
ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA: Open to students
TEAM SIZE: 1-3 members in one team
THE EVENT:
Round 1: Theme exploration and conceptualization (90 mins) The first level kicks off with a burst of creativity as the team embarks on the theme exploration and prototyping phase. In this round, the teams have to develop the initial solution for an existing problem based on the theme given and design the final prototype on any software of choice.
Round 2: Documentation (90 mins) In this round, the team transitions from creative chaos to the documentation phase. This stage requires participants to articulate their ideas with precision. Teams craft a comprehensive document detailing their prototype or concept which will act as a road map explaining the intricacies of the idea, usage, and the problem it aims to solve within the theme. The team must delve into the working of the product and its implementation. Teams are encouraged to add their previous considerations and the potential impact of their idea on the theme. This round will challenge the teams to communicate effectively and incorporate innovation with practicality. The topics to be documented are as follows:
● Defining the problem
● Objective
○ state the specific goals of the prototype.
● Overview of solution
○ Provide an intricate overview of your idea, emphasizing its unique features and benefits.
● Technical and design details
○ Describe the design principle and specifications of your idea. Include diagrams, schematics, and flowcharts.
● Details of Usage
● User interface(UI) and User Experience(UX)
○ (If any) ● Impact of the product on the market.
● Benefits and the mitigations.
● Conclusion
○ Summarise the key points, emphasizing the significance of your idea and its potential impact on the problem.
Round 3: The final showdown (40 mins) The final showdown pushes the team into the realm of marketing magic where they try to transform their ideas into interesting or funny narratives of a TV commercial-type presentation. The challenge is to mix the essence of their concept into a brief yet impactful commercial. This makes the team strategically blend aesthetics, humor, and storytelling to leave a lasting impression on the judges, which will be followed by questions from the judges regarding your detailed documentation which will embrace your team's marketing prowess.
Each level builds on the previous one, creating a dynamic and evolving challenge that tests participants at different stages of the innovation process. This tiered approach ensures a comprehensive evaluation of teams' capabilities, from initial concept development to the refined presentation of their innovative engineering solutions.
SCORING CRITERIA:
The judging criteria for the Pro-Solve Show are:
Round 1: Theme Exploration and Prototyping
1. Creativity and Innovation - 20 points
2. Problem Definition - 20 points
3. Feasibility - 10 points Round 2: Documentation of the Prototype or Concept
1. Comprehensiveness - 20 points
2. Clarity and structure - 20 points
3. Real-world Applicability - 10 points
4. Visuals and presentability - 10 points
Round 3: TV Commercial-style advertisement, Q/A 1. Creativity and Impact - 30 points
2. Product highlight - 30 points
3. Adherence to Time Limit- 10 points
EVENT DURATION: 3-4 hours.

REGISTRATION FEE: 400/-

Team Size: 1-3 members

Rules

1. Any sort of foul play will lead to disqualification of the team.
2. Laptops must be carried by the team.
3. Mobile phone usage is prohibited EXCEPT for the first 10 minutes of Round 1 and Round 2. Laptops must be used for round 1 and round 2.
4. Participants must have basic knowledge of designing software in order to complete round 1.
