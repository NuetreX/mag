Ohm-azing Build-a-thon

Sparking Creativity, Illuminating Minds—Where Circuits Come to Life and Innovation Takes Flight!

The department of EEE presents ‘Ohm-azing Build-a-thon’, a multi-level time-based event that aims to enhance the knowledge in research and development. This event will provide participants with the opportunity to learn about the basics of electrical circuits and how they work, as well as hands-on experience building circuits. There will be 4 circuit building challenges and 1 activity to participate in, such as building a simple circuit that lights up an LED or creating a complex circuit that can control a small motor. Throughout the event, participants will work in small groups to decipher riddles and build the circuits/ideas provided in the event. It will be a fun and engaging way for participants to learn about electrical circuits, and to develop their problem-solving, critical thinking, and teamwork skills. This will be a time-based event with 5 rounds in succession, where points are awarded based on the time taken to complete each circuit. The top 3 teams with the most points at the end of the event will be crowned the winners.

Eligibility Criteria

● Open for all engineering students.
● Students need to have a basic understanding of creating circuits using breadboards, wires etc., electronic concepts and soldering.
● Maximum teams - 10 teams
1 activity + 4 circuit building rounds.
Activity will be for 5 mins. Further details will be given on the day of the event.
First 3 rounds:
● Each circuit building round will be of 15 mins.
● The riddle statement of the circuit will be given at the start of every round.
● The team will have to guess and complete the circuit within 15 minutes. If the team finishes building a working model of the circuit in less than or equal to 10 mins, they will earn 40 points. If the team completes building the circuit in 15 mins, they will earn 30 points (These points are excluded from the points awarded in the guessing period).
● A margin of extra 5 mins will be given for one of the 3 circuit building rounds (Margin of extra 5 mins for one of the rounds will be announced on the day of the event). If the team finishes the circuit within the margin of 5 mins, 20 points will be awarded.
● If the circuit is not completed within the specified time (15 mins), no points will be awarded. Only the points they earned during the guessing period will be considered (i.e., if they manage to score points in the guessing period).
● If the team fails to guess and build the circuit of 1 round, they get a negative marking of -5 points.
The Final Round:
● It will be a maximum of 15 mins where the team will have to completely engage in building the circuit. No guessing period will be given since the clues and if necessary, the circuit diagram, will be given by the event coordinators on the day of the event.

REGISTRATION FEE: 500/-

Team Size: 4-5 members

Rules

● Each team should consist of 4 or 5 members.
● 2 teams will be working simultaneously in two different venues.
● 15 minutes will be given for the completion of each circuit.
● Teams are not allowed to use any of their personal electronic devices.
